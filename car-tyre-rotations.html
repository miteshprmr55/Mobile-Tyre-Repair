FAQs

Tyre rotation refers to the practice of shifting the position of wheels and tyres ensuring even wear and tear. This extends the life and utility of the tyres.

Each tyre on your car supports different weights. This unequal distribution of weight leads to variation in tyre wear. Hence, tyre rotations are essential to achieve even wear for all the tyres and increases their life.

Generally, it is recommended to go for car tyre rotations every 8,000-10,000 km or six months, whichever is earlier

Motorists in Melbourne should go for professional tyre rotations earlier if they:

  • carry heavy loads in their vehicle
  • regularly drive for longer distances
  • drive at high speeds frequently

All these put additional pressure on the tyres causing them to wear even more faster.

A car tyre rotation service involves removing the tyres from a car and reinstalling them to a different position. A tyre may be rotated to a different position based on the tyre wear patterns or a pre-set pattern.

There are several benefits of car tyre rotations. These include:

  • Even wear on all four tyres
  • Prolonged life for all tyres
  • Enhanced safety while driving
  • Vehicle runs seamlessly
  • Save money in the long run
  • Increased fuel efficiency
  • Better vehicle handling and control

Here are some of the warning signs that your car needs professional tyre rotation:

  • Uneven wear patterns on tyres
  • Car pulling to one side
  • Reduced fuel efficiency
  • Steering vibration
  • Poor traction
